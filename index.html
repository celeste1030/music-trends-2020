Analysis of Health Risks based on Income, Age and Poverty

Healthcare: The percentage of people who lack healthcare is most affected by poverty showing that healthcare is a luxury that many Americans unfortunately cannot afford. Median age is similar across all states and does not impact the percentage of people without healthcare. Median income also does not affect lack of healthcare. Texas for example has the highest percentage of people who lack healthcare with a median income of 53,035. Other states such as West Virginia and Kentucky have lower median incomes and lower percentage of lacking healthcare.

Smokers: The percentage of people who are smokers are affected by all three demographics. States with lower median income have a higher percentage of smokers. States with an older median age tend to have more smokers. Increased level of poverty leads to a greater number of smokers.

Obesity: Obesity is most affected by median income. The lower the median income the higher the obesity rate per state. Poverty levels also greatly affect obesity. States with a high poverty level have high obesity rate. Age does not affect obesity since states with similar median age have different obesity levels.
